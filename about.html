Hepatitis

It is a disease that causes inflammation of the liver. The liver is a vital organ responsible for numerous bodily functions, including blood filtration and protein synthesis. Inflammation of the liver impairs its ability to carry out these essential tasks.

-What are the etiologies of hepatitis?

"Viruses are the primary causative agents of hepatitis:"

Hepatitis A virus (HAV)
primarily transmitted via the fecal-oral route

Hepatitis B virus (HBV)
primarily transmitted through blood and bodily fluids

Hepatitis C virus (HCV)
primarily transmitted through blood contact

-What are the clinical manifestations of hepatitis?

1-Dark urine
2-Pale stool
3-Fatigue and tiredness

Alzheimer's disease

"It is Alzheimer's disease, a chronic neurological disease that leads to a gradual deterioration of memory and other cognitive abilities."

Symptoms of Alzheimer's disease:

1-Memory loss
2-Thinking and problem-solving difficulties
3-Language problems
4-Mood swings and behavioral changes
5-Difficulty with daily activities

Prevention of Alzheimer's disease:

1-Regular physical exercise
2-A healthy and balanced diet
3-Maintaining a healthy weight

Anemia

Anemia is a medical condition that occurs when there is a deficiency in the number of healthy red blood cells or in the amount of hemoglobin in the blood. Hemoglobin is a protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen throughout the body. When hemoglobin levels are low, the body does not get enough oxygen, leading to a range of symptoms

Symptoms of anemia:

1-Fatigue and weakness
2-Paleness
3-Shortness of breath
4-Dizziness and lightheadedness
5-Heart palpitations

The treatment of anemia is based on the underlying cause:

1-Iron supplementation
2-Vitamin supplementation
3-Medications

First aid for burns

Burns are frequent injuries that can lead to significant pain and tissue damage. Effective first aid for burns is crucial for minimizing injury severity and preventing secondary complications

To provide first aid for burns, follow these essential steps:

1-Remove the person from the heat source.
2-Cool the burned area.
3-Cover the burn.
4-Do not pop blisters.
5-Do not apply any ointment or cream to the burn.
6-Do not apply any ointment or cream to the burn.
